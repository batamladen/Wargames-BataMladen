Module: Foundations of Stealth

Introduction to Stealth & OPSEC

TL;DR

This lesson introduces the ethical foundations of stealth in cybersecurity — what stealth means, how OPSEC principles apply to ethical research, and how defenders detect stealthy activity. You'll also learn how to safely build your own isolated lab environment for hands-on practice.

Why Study Stealth?

  • Understanding stealthy behavior helps defenders improve detection, containment, and response.
  • Ethical research into stealth supports both red and blue team skill development.

Important: This material is strictly for authorized, ethical, and defensive use. Never apply these ideas on systems you don't own or have explicit permission to test.

OPSEC Basics

  • Least Exposure: minimize the information and artifacts you leave behind during experiments.
  • Isolation: separate test environments from production systems.
  • Authorization: always operate within approved scope and written permission.
  • Minimal Impact: test safely and avoid real-world disruption.
  • Documentation: keep records of your steps for transparency and accountability.

Stealth Goals

  • Avoiding detection by EDR, IDS, or logging tools.
  • Reducing artifacts left on disk or memory.
  • Blending in with normal traffic or user behavior.
  • Maintaining long-term presence without triggering alerts.

Defenders counter these goals by collecting telemetry and building behavioral baselines to detect anomalies.

How Defenders Detect Stealth

  • Telemetry-first: gathering logs from endpoints, networks, and authentication systems.
  • Baseline detection: understanding "normal" to find "abnormal."
  • Correlation: combining multiple data sources to reduce false positives.
  • Hunting: forming hypotheses and verifying them with forensic analysis.

Threat Modeling Exercise

Create a short one-page threat model for a small company scenario:

  • Assets: list what systems or data matter most.
  • Adversary: what skills or resources do they have?
  • Stealth goals: what kind of hidden activity might they attempt?
  • Defender signals: which logs or sensors could expose them?
  • Boundaries: what's in-scope and what's not?

Safe Lab Setup

  • Use isolated virtual machines with no access to production systems.
  • Create snapshots before testing, so you can revert changes safely.
  • Keep your lab offline or simulate Internet access locally.
  • Log your actions and timestamps for every experiment.

Resources Covering this Topic

  • "The Art of Cyber Defense and Detection" – a guide to modern defensive operations.
  • MITRE ATT&CK Framework – understanding adversary techniques and detection coverage.
  • OWASP Testing Guide – safe and ethical web security testing practices.
  • US-CERT Operational Security Guidelines – principles for protecting sensitive operations.

Key Takeaway

Stealth differs from each sides perspective:
Offense (Red Team): is about hiding malicious intent and avoid being detected.
Defense (Blue Team): is about understanding how attackers evade detection so they can build stronger defenses.
